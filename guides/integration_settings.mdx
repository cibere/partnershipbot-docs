## What are integrations?

In general, integrations are applications with slash commands. This is normally in the form of a traditional discord bot.

## Opening the integration settings

<Note>This is only accessable from the desktop app or the website</Note>

### 1. Open the server settings

![open server settings](https://i.imgur.com/SBe9iMf.png)

### 2. Open the integration settings

![Go to `Apps` -> `Integrations`](https://i.imgur.com/V7bI6eD.png)

### 3. Select the bot

![Click the `PartnershipBot` card](https://i.imgur.com/mlG3eHM.png)

## Editing The Integration Settings

You have two main types of command overrides

### Adding overrides to all commands

![Image](https://i.imgur.com/jwJKRiQ.png)

#### By role/member

Click `Add Roles or Members`, and use the search bar to find the role/member. Once you've found them, use the checkbox on the left of them to enable/disable it. Press save to save your changes.

<Note>
  If `@everyone` is enabled, role/member overrides will not do anything
  <Expandable title="Image">
    ![Image](https://i.imgur.com/ujN6g8F.png)
  </Expandable>
</Note>

#### By channel

Click `Add Channels`, and use the search bar to find the channel. Once you've found it, use the checkbox on the left of them to enable/disable it. Press save to save your changes.

<Note>
  If `All Channels` is enabled, channel overrides will not do anything
  <Expandable title="Image">
    ![Image](https://i.imgur.com/q0iiWWl.png)
  </Expandable>
</Note>
